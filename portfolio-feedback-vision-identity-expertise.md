# Feedback: Vision & Identity (updated)
### Against FMP Rubrics pp. 117–120 (3.1 Professional Identity, 3.2 Vision)

This replaces the earlier review now that both sections have real content. Identity in particular has changed a lot since the last pass, in good ways: it now names a real weakness with a concrete project example, ties the design process to a named method, and gives the values paragraph more texture. Below is where each rubric sub-item stands now, plus what's still open.

## 3.1 Professional Identity

**3.1.A (steers work and career, shows development, connects to vision and goals).** Still the strongest item, and now stronger. The arc from 3D-printing/Voron, to the curiosity-driven Research through Design approach, to Aqua-Morph (now explicitly tied to the Material Driven Design method), to the Preparation Project pitfall, to learning co-design for the thesis, to Tabula, reads as genuine development rather than a list of projects. The one piece still missing is the career-facing half of this item, covered under 3.1.E below.

**3.1.B (strengths AND weaknesses, across designerly skill, design process, teamwork, AND the Expertise Area framework).** This moved the most. The Preparation Project paragraph now names a real, specific weakness, that a first-person making instinct can mean under-engaging with outside experts, and follows it with a real response: deliberately learning the co-design method for the thesis. That's exactly the strength-and-weakness, before-and-after structure this item is looking for, and it touches design process (RtD, iterative testing), skill (3D printing and materials knowledge, embedded electronics), and a teamwork-adjacent element (reaching out to experts, practising co-design with users) all at once. The one element still absent is the Expertise Area framework itself, since that page is still empty. Once that page exists, even a short cross-reference back to this paragraph (e.g., "this is where I'm weaker on X expertise area") would complete all four elements.

**3.1.C (beliefs/norms/values connected to design activities).** The values paragraph is more developed now, the bug-fix/community point and the Prusa vs. Bambu Lab example give it real texture, and the closing line ("it's also made me reconsider my approach") gestures at an active, ongoing connection rather than a one-off post-hoc link. But that closing line is also where it stops short: it doesn't say what changed. Right now an assessor can credit "beliefs connected to design activities," but probably not "integrally drive the design activities," because the one place that would prove it doesn't say what the reconsidered approach actually looks like in practice. Naming one concrete thing, e.g. how you license, document, or share Tabula differently because of this, would be a small addition with a real payoff here.

**3.1.D (corresponding visual language) and 3.1.E (career path matching PI).** 3.1.D still depends on the site's visual presentation, not assessed in this pass. 3.1.E is still a clean gap: nothing on the page addresses a career path or market/academic position, and `future.md` is still empty and off the main menu. Since this is scored Yes/No, it's worth treating as a real to-do rather than a nice-to-have.

## 3.2 Vision

**3.2.A and 3.2.B (critical positioning; personal experience as integral).** Unchanged in substance from the last review and still solid: the literature grounding (Troiano, Boem, Mudd) gives the critique real teeth, and the personal guitar-to-production anecdote is now live in the text, giving 3.2.B the grounding it was missing before.

**3.2.C (path to realizing the vision, points of action, societal/knowledge impact).** This is the other big improvement. The new closing sentence, framing music as a safe testbed whose insights transfer to other domains (gaming, automotive), is a real point of action and a real impact claim where before the section just trailed off. This is now reasonably well covered.

**3.2.D (vision consistently communicated across attitude, work, and other communication).** Still depends on consistency across the whole site/presentation, not something this page alone can settle.

## One factual addition made while editing

The Aqua-Morph paragraph had a `[CITE]` placeholder for the Material Driven Design method. I looked it up and replaced it with the actual source: Karana, E., Barati, B., Rognoli, V., & Zeeuw van der Laan, A. (2015), "Material driven design (MDD): A method to design for material experiences," *International Journal of Design*, 9(2), 35-54. Worth a quick check that this is the paper you had in mind before it ships, since I inserted it rather than asking first.

The Tabula project/thesis link mentioned in earlier notes is still missing from Identity. And 4.1 (Integration of expertise areas) is unchanged from the last review, still entirely dependent on the empty Expertise Areas page.
